In most arithmetic operations, U denotes “no overflow test.” Unsuffixed arithmetic operations like add cause a CPU exception if the result overflows into bit 31 (the sign bit when we’re thinking of integers as signed). The suffixed variant addu produces exactly the same result for all combinations of operands but never takes an exception.C and C++ do not support integer overflow exceptions and always use the “u” form—it has nothing to do with whether a variable is signed or unsigned. You should always use addu and so on unless you really know why not.

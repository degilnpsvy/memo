8.7.6 Integer Multiply-Accumulate

Some MIPS CPUs have various forms of integer multiply-accumulate instructions—none of them in a MIPS standard instruction set. All these instructions take two general-purpose registers and accumulate into lo and hi. As usual, “u” denotes an unsigned variant, but otherwise the mnemonic (and instruction code) is specific to a particular CPU implementation.

dmadd16, madd16: Specific to NEC Vr4100, these variants gain speed by only accepting 16-bit operands, making them of very limited use to a C compiler.

dmadd16 accumulates a 64-bit result in the 64-bit lo register.

mad, madu: Found in Toshiba R3900, IDT R4640/4650, and QED CPUs, these take two 32-bit operands and accumulate a 64-bit result split between the lo and hi registers. TheToshiba R3900 allows a three-operand version, mad d,s,t, in which the accumulated value is also transferred to the general-purpose register d.

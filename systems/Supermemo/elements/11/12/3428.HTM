But the assembler found in most toolchains is likely to leave this to the compiler. By a less-than-obvious convention, a multiply or divide written with the destination register zero (as in div zero,s,t) will give you the raw machine instruction.（Some toolkits interpret special mnemonics, mult for multiplication and divd for division, for the machine instructions. However, specifying zero as the destination, though bizarre, is more portable.）It is then up to you to fetch the result from hi and/or lo and to do any checking you need.

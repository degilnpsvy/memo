If you want to avoid clobbering your system log with the monitoring messages from your driver, you can either specify the –f (file) option to klogd to instruct it to save messages to a specific file, or customize /etc/syslog.conf to suit your needs. Yet another possibility is to take the brute-force approach: kill klogd and verbosely print messages on an unused virtual terminal,*( * For example, use setlevel 8; setconsole 10 to set up terminal 10 to display messages.) or issue the command cat /proc/kmsg from an unused xterm.

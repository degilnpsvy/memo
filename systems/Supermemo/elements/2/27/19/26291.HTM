The only peculiarity is that a handler runs at interrupt time and, therefore, suffers some restrictions on what it can do. These restrictions are the same as those we saw with kernel timers.

 A handler can’t transfer data to or from user space, because it doesn’t execute in the context of a process.

Handlers also cannot do anything that would sleep, such as calling wait_event,

allocating memory with anything other than GFP_ATOMIC,

or locking a semaphore.

Finally, handlers cannot call schedule.

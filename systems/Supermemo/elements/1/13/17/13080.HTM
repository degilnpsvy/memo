9.2. Push and Pop

The push() and pop() macros are used to save and restore the contents of non-temporary registers. All non-temporary registers used by a subprogram or macro must be restored to their original value before returning to the caller.

The details of this are not important at this stage, and will be discussed in Chapter 10, Subprogram Calls and the Stack.

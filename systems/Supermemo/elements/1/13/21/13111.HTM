13.1. Motivation

Logic and shift operations are an important part of every computer architecture. Although their uses may not be apparent to the average application programmer, they are used heavily in every computer system for managing I/O devices, converting between various data types, and encrypting and decrypting data. They are also used occasionally for improving performance of multiplication and division.

The use of bit operations for I/O is demonstrated in Chapter 14, Input/Output and Chapter 15, Exceptions.

13.4. Homework

  1. Where are logic and shift operations used most?

  2. Show the binary result of each of the following:

    • 10010010 ^ 01011111
    • 10010100 v 00100011
    • 10011100'
    • 00010110 xor 11111111

  3. Write MAL instructions to perform each of the following tasks:

    • Clear the rightmost 4 bits of $s0.
    • Set the rightmost 14 bits of $s0, placing the result in $s2.
    • Toggle bits 4-7 of $a0.

  4. Write a sequence of MAL instructions that computes (a ^ (b v 0x000F145A)) xor (c' ^ 0xFF000000)

  5. Explain the difference between a 1-bit logical shift and a 1-bit arithmetic shift right.

  6. How are logical and arithmetic shifts used with integer data?

  7. Perform the following shift operations:

    • FC10 logical shift right 4 bits.
    • FC10 arithmetic shift right 4 bits.
    • FC10 rotate left 8 bits.
    • FC10 rotate right 2 bits.

  8. Write a sequence of MAL instructions to multiply the value in $t0 by 10. ( Hint, what powers of 2 add up to 10? )

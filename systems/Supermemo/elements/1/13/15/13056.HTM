5.8. Homework

  1. Describe three reasons for studying computer organization and assembly language.

  2. Explain the difference between CPU architecture and implementation.

  3. Draw a diagram showing the major components of a CPU and how they are connected.

  4. Show how the word 04F2E59116 would be stored in byte-addressable memory at address FFFF0000 using the following byte orderings:

    • Big-endian
    • Little-endian

  5. What are registers, and why are they necessary?

  6. What is the program counter?

  7. What is the instruction register?

  8. What is the control unit?

  9. What is the ALU?

  10. Where does the ALU get the operands for the operations it performs, and where does it store the results?

  11. What is the advantage of memory-to-memory architectures over other architecture types?

  12. What is the advantage of load-store architectures over memory-to-memory or register-memory architectures?

  13. Explain the difference between Von Neumann and Harvard architectures. Where are Harvard architectures popular, and why?

  14. What are the characteristics that distinguish CISC from RISC architectures?

  15. Describe each step of the generic instruction cycle.

  16. Describe the major groups of MIPS general-purpose registers.

  17. Explain the difference between a register instruction and an immediate instruction.

  18. What is an assembler directive?

  19. What is a typeless language?

  20. Write a MAL program that computes the area of a circle, given the radius as input from the standard input.

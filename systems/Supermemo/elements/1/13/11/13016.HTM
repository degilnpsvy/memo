1.4. Learning to Succeed

1.4.1. The Learning Process

Learning literally involves re-wiring your brain. New long-term memory and understanding comes from growing new connections between axons and dendrites. This is a biological process that occurs at a fairly slow, constant pace. You cannot hurry up and learn. The best you can do in a hurry is cram things into short term memory, from which they will soon vanish.

True learning requires repeated exposure to the stimuli that cause new connections to form. The more frequently you think about something, the sooner you will master it. Hence, studying or doing homework in one marathon session is a strategy doomed to failure. You will not retain much of what you do. Work on homework and review notes a little every day, and you will have a much easier time mastering the material and retaining it long-term.

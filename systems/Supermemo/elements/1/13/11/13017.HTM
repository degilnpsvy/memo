1.5. Success in College

1.5.1. Grades

What if your instructor canceled class for the rest of the semester and gave everyone an A? Would you be happy? Well served? Ready for the workplace?

What if your college gave out degrees for cash? Pay $30,000 and you get a computer science diploma, no work involved. What would this diploma do for you?

Grades don't make you successful, skills do. In particular, the ability to learn quickly and independently is by far the most important. This is the one skill that allows you to accomplish anything that you really want to.

Most of your life, you've been bombarded with well-intentioned but bad advice, such as “keep your grades up”. The problem is, the goal of getting good grades does not lead to the same actions or results as the goal of building skills and understanding. Striving to develop strong skills and depth of understanding will lead to good grades, while the converse is not necessarily true.

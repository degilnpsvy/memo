3.1.1 Polygonal and Polyhedral Models

In this and the next subsection, a solid representation of will be developed in terms of a combination of primitives. Each primitive represents a subset of that is easy to represent and manipulate in a computer. A complicated obstacle region will be represented by taking finite, Boolean combinations of primitives. Using set theory, this implies that can also be defined in terms of a finite number of unions, intersections, and set differences of primitives.

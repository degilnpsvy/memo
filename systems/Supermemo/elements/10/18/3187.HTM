For diagnostic and maintenance purposes it’s good to be able to read and write the cache tags; MIPS32/64 defines a pair of 32-bit registers TagLo and TagHi(Some CPU manuals use different registers to talk to the I- and D-caches, and then call them ITagLo, and so on.) to stage data between the tag part of the cache and management software. TagHi is often not necessary: Until your physical address space is more than 36 bits, there is usually room for the whole tag in TagLo.

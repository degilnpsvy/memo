You really can get rid of the explicit invalidations and write-backs by accessing all the memory used for all DMA transfers via an uncached address region. This isn’t recommended either, because it’ll almost certainly degrade your system’s overall performance far more than you’d like. Even if your software’s access to the buffers is purely sequential, caching the DMA buffer regions will mean that information gets read and written in efficient cache-line-sized bursts rather than single transfers.
